The Core Idea

The Legal Compass is a beautifully designed mobile app that gives travellers, expats, and globally mobile professionals a clear, plain-English guide to their legal rights and key laws in any country — before they land, and when they need it most.

Think Citizens Advice meets Lonely Planet meets a brilliant lawyer friend who answers in plain English. Switch countries instantly. Get a traffic-light risk overview. Know what to do in an emergency. Find vetted local legal help when you need it.

Crucially: this is legal information, not legal advice — a critical distinction that keeps the model safe. Every piece of content signposts to official government sources and links to verified local lawyers when the user needs more.

The Gap

Citizens Advice is UK-only and text-heavy. Gov.uk travel advisories cover safety but not legal rights. Law firms have no consumer product. Legal Zoom is US contracts only. Nobody owns the "global legal awareness for regular people" space. It's wide open.

🚦 The Traffic Light Content Model

Every country profile uses a simple three-tier system that helps users instantly understand their situation — and keeps the app safely in "information" territory, not "advice."

🟢 Know Before You Go
General legal context, cultural norms with legal implications, rights you have as a visitor, what to carry (ID requirements), local emergency numbers.
e.g. "In the UK, you have the right to remain silent if arrested." · "In Japan, carry your passport at all times."
🟡 Worth Being Aware
Laws that commonly catch tourists or expats off-guard. Not necessarily dangerous, but worth knowing. Linked to official government sources for verification.
e.g. "In Singapore, chewing gum is restricted." · "In Thailand, it's illegal to step on currency."
🔴 Critical Alerts
Laws with severe penalties, rights violations that are documented, or situations requiring immediate legal help. Direct link to local vetted lawyers and embassy contacts.
e.g. "Thailand: lèse-majesté — criticism of the monarchy is a criminal offence." · "Drug possession penalties in this country include life imprisonment."

🛡️ Liability Framework — How We Stay Safe

Risk Level Mitigation
User acts on content and suffers harm Medium Prominent "information not advice" framing throughout. Onboarding disclaimer. Every article links to official gov source.
Content becomes outdated / inaccurate High Quarterly content review cycle. Date-stamped articles. "Last verified" shown on every page. User reporting tool.
Unauthorised practice of law Medium Strict editorial policy: describe the law, never apply it to a specific situation. Legal review of editorial guidelines before launch.
Country sensitivity / diplomatic risk Low Factual, neutral tone. Cite official sources only. No editorial opinion on governments. Amnesty International model.
PI insurance availability Medium Obtain from day one. Budget ~$2–5K/year for a startup. Becca's legal background strengthens the application.
GDPR / data privacy Low Minimal personal data collected. Clear privacy policy. No selling of user data.
✅ The golden rule: We describe what the law is. We never say what someone should do in their specific situation. That line, maintained consistently, is what keeps this app in the clear.
