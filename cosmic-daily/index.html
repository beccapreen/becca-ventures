The Core Idea

A beautifully designed iPhone app (and lock screen widget) that gives you a daily spiritual weather report — combining lunar cycles, key astrological events, and grounded daily guidance into something you can glance at before you even get out of bed.

Not a full astrology app. Not a horoscope generator. Something more personal, more poetic, and more useful — like having a wise, calm friend who knows the cosmos and keeps you anchored.

The lock screen widget means the guidance is ambient — you absorb it naturally, the way you absorb the weather, without needing to open an app or do anything. It meets you where you already are.

The Gap in the Market

Existing apps (Co-Star, The Pattern, Chani) are either too dense, too dark, or require too much prior astrological knowledge. None of them prioritise the lockscreen / ambient experience. There is a huge underserved audience who are spiritually curious but want something grounding and ADHD-friendly — not overwhelming.

✦ Core Features

🔒
Lock Screen Widget
Moon phase, today's cosmic weather tag, and a single affirmation — visible the moment you pick up your phone. iOS 16+ Live Activity support. Customisable size and style.
🌕
Lunar Calendar
Full moon countdown, new moon intentions prompt, eclipse alerts, and lunar phase descriptions with practical guidance for each phase. Monthly lunar calendar view.
⚡
Cosmic Weather Feed
Plain-English summaries of major transits — Mercury Retrograde, Neptune seasons, Venus cazimi, eclipses. No jargon. Just "what does this mean for me today?"
🌅
Daily Guidance Card
A fresh daily card combining the cosmic weather into practical advice: what to focus on, what to avoid, where your energy is best directed. Grounded and actionable.
🌿
Affirmations & Prompts
Daily affirmation aligned to the cosmic season. Optional journalling prompt for reflection. Morning and evening modes. ADHD-friendly: one clear message, not a wall of text.
♑
Birth Chart Personalisation
Optional: input your sun/moon/rising signs to personalise the guidance. Guidance shifts based on how current transits interact with your chart. Depth for those who want it.

✦ What Makes It Different

🔒
Lock Screen First
No competitor owns the lock screen. This app is designed to be ambient — you see it before you check anything else. That's a different relationship with guidance.
🌍
Grounded, Not Woo
Spiritual but practical. Each cosmic event translates to a clear action: what to do, what to pause, where to focus. Never scary, never vague. Useful first.
🧠
ADHD-Friendly Design
One message per day. Clean layout. No information overload. Designed for people who need clarity, not complexity. Big underserved audience.
🌙
Plain English Cosmic Weather
No prerequisite astrological knowledge required. "Mercury is retrograde — here's what that means today" not "Mercury squares Saturn in Capricorn."

✦ Questions for You

01
Which spiritual traditions feel most authentic to you — Western astrology, lunar/pagan cycles, Buddhist/Vedic, a blend? This shapes the whole tone and content.
→ Determines whether we lean Celtic wheel, Vedic astrology, Western tropical, or a modern eclectic blend.
02
How personal do you want the guidance to be? Purely cosmic (universal for everyone) or personalised by birth chart (sun/moon/rising)?
→ Personalisation adds huge value but also complexity. Could be a free vs premium differentiator.
03
What's the emotional tone you want the app to strike? Options: calm & meditative / warm & encouraging / wise & poetic / practical & direct — or a mix?
→ Defines the writing voice for all affirmations, guidance cards, and cosmic summaries.
04
Is this something you'd want to build yourself (with Sean's help), license the concept, or explore as a side business? That changes how deep we go.
→ Shapes whether we go to MVP prototype, pitch deck, or full product spec.
05
Beyond astrology, are there other systems you'd want included — numerology, Human Design, tarot archetypes, Gene Keys, moon mansions?
→ Each system has a passionate following and could expand the addressable market significantly.
06
Who is the primary user in your mind — someone like you (ambitious, ADHD-friendly, grounding needed), or broader? And do you see this as a solo tool or something with a community/sharing element?
→ Determines UX priorities and whether social features (sharing your daily card, etc.) are worth building.
